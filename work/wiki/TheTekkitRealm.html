    “ My name is Dallas A.K.A Infurness… ”

    ―TheTekkitRealm

    Dallas Korol (born: November 5, 2000 (2000-11-05) [age 20]), better known online as TheTekkitRealm (also known as Infurness), is an American YouTuber, gamer and commentator known for his "1-year" streams (which he recently stopped doing) and random video style. His original main purpose was to be a giant let's play channel for the popular game Minecraft. In his time on YouTube, he has made several channels with several styles, a video streaming platform, music and other things. Dallas also used his channel for a school project when he was 14. It was later named "TheTekkitRealm" after the name "TheTek" was already taken.

    History

    Dallas has gone by many names and alter egos. As stated in his video, "My YouTube Story..." [2] on December 24, 2012, Dallas created his first channel, later to go by the name of Glasszombie. Soon after accumulating 7,000 subscribers on two channels, they were both terminated under false copyright strikes. He decided to ditch YouTube altogether and pursue web development. This led to the creation of Dallas' website. Dallas has gained a reputation from videos, and live streams. Most particularly involving PewDiePie vs T-Series. He has recently started working on a new series called "YouTube Unsolved" where he attempts to solve the "mysteries of YouTube".

    Dallas has a music channel named DKVibez, containing Chillstep Trap and other genres of music.

    YouTube

    Dallas started out as a channel that mainly focused on random video topics and original ideas. Due to the algorithm change in mid-2016, Dallas has mentioned how he now has to upload a more specific brand of content to continue growing and can be quite demotivating how only "red arrows" and clickbait seem to attract attention nowadays. Thus branding the "Not Clickbait" inside joke of the channel and he gets about 30K subscribers a week. His YouTube channel is described as a "private, not-for-profit educational and entertainment brand". [3]

    1 Year Streams

    Dallas has grown notorious for multiple 1 year live streams. Dallas' most popular livestream is "Playing Subway Surfers For 1 Year (World Record)" which to date has achieved over 30 million views.

    YouTube Unsolved

    On March 10, 2020, TheTekkitRealm started a series called YouTube Unsolved. Each episode is dedicated to uncovering supposedly unsolved and notorious YouTube mysteries. One of its most known episodes revolves around the "Username 666" mystery.

    Criticism

    Faking Videos and Livestreams

    TheTekkitRealm has been accused by multiple people for faking his videos and some of his one year livestreams, particularly some of the YouTube Unsolved videos. Examples of this include:

    His video on COM-TEST showed many fake/non-existent Reddit accounts[4][5][6] (only one of them is real and conveniently has no post history) and posts, and the screenshots shown by these posts display several UI inconsistencies.[7][8][9][10][11]

    His video on PieSquared showed screenshots showing impossible SocialBlade behavior. The screenshots showed PieSquared as the number one channel on Social Blade with a rank of A++ which isn't possible since SocialBlade ranks most high-subscriber channels without videos with a low rank[12], and PieSquared had no videos when the video was made.

    His video on 2020 VISION including fake/non-existent info supporting TheTekkitRealm's claim of 2020 VISION leading a real cult, such as a fake DM between "Jerry" and 2020 VISION, several critical misunderstandings of how 2020 VISION's Discord works and several logic flaws.

    His video on the Fanultima Network claiming that TheTekkitRealm was emailed by "Jeff Watson" of the "Fanultima Network". The "Fanultima Network" doesn't exist and "Jeff Watson" is actually Thomas Watson[13], one of TheTekkitRealm's many sockpuppet accounts[13][14][15]. The Thomas Watson account was originally made to fake a 5-star review of his own house[14][15][16]. (Reference 15 proves Reference 14, as "John Wilson" also gave his house a 5-star review)

    A livestream where he claimed to mine in a straight line in Minecraft for one year. The specific auto clicker he used clicks in a way that makes mining impossible, not only that but the footage in the stream is looped[17][18].

    Stealing Gameplay

    TheTekkitRealm has been accused of stealing gameplay and content.[19] One example of this is TheTekkitRealm's video on a Russian YouTube channel that faked 10 million subscribers using Coopydood's Minecraft Dungeon beta gameplay uncredited. In TheTekkitRealm's video you can even see Coopydood's old logo (pixel art of a smiling Mac).

    Silencing Criticism and Critics

    When Guvoid made his now infamous video on TheTekkitRealm, many of TheTekkitRealm's fans would find the video, agree with Guvoid, and share the video with other fans, usually in the YouTube Unsolved Discord server. On May 11, 2021, TheTekkitRealm would say this to his Discord staff: "New staff based guideline. Users that come into this server immediately proclaiming that "thetekkitrealm" or "Youtube unsolved" is fake is now a bannable offense. Considering this is to remain a positive Nondefamitory community about mysteries and nothing more, there is absolutely no reason for this, as it is extremely repetitive, incorrect and annoying. This should also be updated into the public rules if possible. Thanks!"[20] This rule was unofficially implemented immediately although it would not be added to the rules directly, instead a rule preventing the spread of Guvoid's video was implemented[21].

    On May 3, 2021, an early version of the Criticism section would be added to this article. On May 6, TheTekkitRealm would contact Wikitubia Staff to remove all criticism from his article.

    Career

    He has recorded podcasts with Zach King on YouTube topics such as Attention People, which was released in November 2018, in which he discussed his view on the YouTube Algorithm,[22] stating; I now have to upload a more specific brand of content to continue growing and can be quite demotivating how only "red arrows" and clickbait seem to attract attention nowadays. Thus branding the "Not Clickbait" inside joke of the channel.

    TheTekkitRealm has also produced documentary style movies on its channel, most notably THE RAID - Official Area 51 Documentary, which was a documentative film about the famous "Storm Area 51, They Can't Stop All of Us" Facebook event.[23]

    TheTekkitRealm owns his own YouTube network called TheTekkitRealm Network. The network consists of 9 Youtubers (excluding TheTekkitRealm). TheTekkitRealm is also the owner of his own corporation called TheTekkitRealm Corp.[24]

    TheTekkitRealm has also made ventures into video streaming. He claims that his website is "a decentralized website designed for the video streaming industry".[25] Dallas explains that his goals are to "enable them (viewers) to engage more actively with video platforms and content creators"[25] and to "create the first Decentralized Video Streaming and Delivery Network"[25].

    Trivia

    • Dallas has been fully active since the beginning on August 7, 2015, although the website has been up and running since mid 2014.
    • As of 2019, Dallas receives an average of 2700 donations, support funds, and super chats every 30 days.[3]
